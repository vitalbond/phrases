<div><p>W3C announced today a new <a href="http://www.w3.org/Payments/">Web Payments
Initiative</a> to integrate payments seamlessly into the Open Web
Platform. W3C calls upon all industry stakeholders –banks,
credit card companies, governments, mobile network operators, payment
solution providers, technology companies, retailers, and content
creators– to join the <a href="http://www.w3.org/Payments/IG/">new Payments Interest Group</a>
and leverage the unique ability of the Web to bridge ecosystem
diversity and reach users everywhere, on any device. The result will
be new business opportunities, an improved user experience for online
transactions, reduced fraud, and increased interoperability among
traditional solutions and future payment innovations.  Read the <a href="http://www.w3.org/2014/10/payments.html.en">full press release</a> and <a href="http://www.w3.org/2014/10/payments.html.en#testimonials">testimonials from W3C
Members</a>, including Bloomberg, Gemalto, GRIN Technologies, 
Ingenico Group, NACS, Rabobank, and Yandex.</p></div>
