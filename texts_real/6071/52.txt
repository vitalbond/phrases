<h3>Supreme Court Puts Hold on Same-Sex Marriages in Virginia</h3>
<p>A day before same-sex couples were slated to begin marrying in Virginia, <a title="the U.S. Supreme Court delayed" href="http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2014/08/20/supreme-court-virginia-gay-marriage_n_5695768.html" target="_blank">the U.S. Supreme Court delayed</a> an appeal’s court rule striking down the state’s gay marriage ban.  The decision was not unexpected, and like other southern states, Virginia is attempting to strike a balance between traditional, conservative values of the south and modern times.  17% of news about the hold on same-sex marriage was shared on Twitter.</p>
<h3>Don Pardo, TV and Radio Announcer, Dies at 96</h3>
<p>You may not recognize Don Pardo’s face, but you definitely would recognize his voice.  The radio and television announcer most know for his lineup introductions on <em>Saturday Night Live</em> <a title="passed away on Monday" href="http://newyork.cbslocal.com/2014/08/19/radio-tv-announcer-don-pardo-dies/" target="_blank">passed away on Monday</a> at the age of 96.  News of his death spread quickly through Facebook, which made up 63% of shared content.</p>
<h3>USDA: Cost to Raise a Child up Slightly to $245,340</h3>
<p>The USDA released his annual report this week on the <a title="cost of raising a child up to the age of 18" href="http://abcnews.go.com/Health/wireStory/report-cost-raise-todays-child-tops-245000-25019405" target="_blank">cost of raising a child up to the age of 18</a>.  Excluding college expenses, a child born in 2013 will cost a middle-income family an average of $245,340.  This number is a 1.8% increase over last year’s number.</p>
<h3>Escaped Ebola Patients Found as Outbreak Death Toll Tops 1,200</h3>
<p>The Ebola outbreak was a hot topic this week, and 23% of content about Ebola was shared via Twitter.  A raid of an Ebola center in West Point, Liberia on Saturday allowed for <a title="37 infected patients to flee" href="http://abcnews.go.com/Health/escaped-ebola-patients-found-outbreak-death-toll-tops/story?id=25034637#1" target="_blank">37 infected patients to flee</a>, but all patients have since been found.  In more positive news, the <a title="two Americans being treated" href="http://www.usatoday.com/story/news/nation/2014/08/21/ebola-us-patients-brantly-writebol-atlanta/14376997/" target="_blank">two Americans being treated</a> for Ebola were released this week.</p>
<h3>Majors that Can Lead to the Highest-Paying Jobs</h3>
<p>Back-to-school is right around the corner, and college students should choose their majors wisely.  CareerCast released an infographic of the <a title="top degrees of 2014 and best-paying jobs of 2014" href="http://abcnews.go.com/Business/highest-earning-majors-back-school/story?id=24961735" target="_blank">top degrees of 2014 and the best-paying jobs of 2014</a>.   Topping the list were business, statistics, computer engineering, and chemistry-related fields.</p>
