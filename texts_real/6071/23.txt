<h3>Hobby Lobby Wins Contraceptive Ruling in Supreme Court</h3>
<p>Under a part of ObamaCare, employers had to offer contraceptive coverage to employees. Because of religious beliefs, Hobby Lobby did not believe they should have to cover certain forms of birth control. <a title="The Supreme Court ruled" href="http://abcnews.go.com/Politics/hobby-lobby-wins-contraceptive-ruling-supreme-court/story?id=24364311" target="_blank">The Supreme Court ruled</a> that for-profit companies should not have to pay for something they’re against because of religious objections. Over 20% of content shared about the ruling was shared to Twitter.</p>
<h3>Social Media to be Granted an Emoji-Only Network</h3>
<p>Emojis have become a popular way of communicating via text message, and social media is now taking it to a new level. The <a title="online network Emojli" href="http://www.socialmediafrontiers.com/2014/07/social-media-to-be-granted-emoji-only.html#.U7V2jI2f8nY" target="_blank">online network Emojli</a> will eliminate words, and even your username will be made out of animated pictures, which you can <a title="reserve here" href="http://emoj.li/" target="_blank">reserve here</a>. Word of the new network spread most frequently through Twitter, making up over 50% of shared content.</p>
<h3>Belgium Holds on to Beat US 2-1 in Extra Time</h3>
<p>Team USA’s goalkeeper made the most saves in a match in World Cup history. Despite the team’s best efforts, <a title="Belgium won the match 2-1" href="http://cajavila.com/post/sports/belgium-holds-on-to-beat-us-2-1-in-extra-time-yahoo-sports/" target="_blank">Belgium won the match 2-1</a>, eliminating Team USA from the World Cup. Belgium’s victory allows them to advance to the quarterfinal match this Saturday against Argentina.</p>
<h3>‘Like a Girl’ is No Longer an Insult in P&#38;G’s New Ad</h3>
<p>“Like a girl” is a common insult thrown around on playgrounds and in schools. <a title="Procter &#38; Gamble's new ad" href="http://www.adweek.com/adfreak/girl-no-longer-insult-inspiring-ad-pgs-always-158601" target="_blank">Procter &#38; Gamble’s new ad</a> is aiming to spin the insult into a source of power for girls. The ad shows young girls running and fighting “like a girl” with purpose and power.  Girls’ confidence typically drops in puberty, but this empowering ad hopefully shifts the idea that “like a girl” can be a good thing!</p>
