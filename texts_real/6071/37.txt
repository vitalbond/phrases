<h3>Comic-Con Exclusives: What you May Have Missed</h3>
<p><a title="San Diego Comic-Con" href="http://perezhilton.com/2014-07-28-comic-con-recap-exclusive-reign-originals-vampire-diaries-gifs" target="_blank">San Diego Comic-Con</a>, the entertainment and comic convention held annually, took place over the weekend. The convention usually fills the San Diego Convention Center to capacity and has 130,000 attendees. Attendees usually dress in costume and see previews of upcoming films, review their portfolios with top comic book companies, and attend panels. Comic-Con was most talked about on Facebook, with <strong>38% of shared content</strong> being distributed to this service.</p>
<h3>American Doctor in Africa Tests Positive for Ebola</h3>
<p>Ebola has killed nearly 700 people in African countries since the outbreak began earlier this year. There is no cure for the highly contagious virus, and it can kill up to 90% of people infected. An <a title="American doctor tested positive for Ebola" href="http://abcnews.go.com/US/wireStory/american-doctor-africa-tests-positive-ebola-24729144" target="_blank">American doctor tested positive for Ebola</a> over the weekend, following the trend that casualties of such outbreaks tend to be healthcare workers who car for infected individuals. Information about the ebola outbreak has spread mostly through Facebook, making up <strong>52% of shared content.</strong></p>
<h3><em>Hunger Games: Mockingjay Part 1</em> Trailer</h3>
<p>The trailer for <em><a title="The Hunger Games: Mockingjay Part 1" href="http://screenrant.com/hunger-games-mockingjay-part-1-trailer-sdcc-2014/" target="_blank">The Hunger Games: Mockingjay Part 1</a> </em>was released this week, to the excitement of fans who have been waiting anxiously since last November’s <em>The Hunger Games: Catching Fire</em>. The trailer follows the first portion of Susan Collins’ book by the same name. Users shared information about the trailer to Twitter, making up <strong>28% of shared content.</strong></p>
<h3>Ad Creatives Launch ‘ManServants,’ Offering Hunks for Hire</h3>
<p>A new company called <a title="ManServants" href="http://www.adweek.com/adfreak/ad-creatives-launch-manservants-offering-hunks-hire-who-arent-just-strippers-159158" target="_blank">ManServants,</a> which is set to launch in the fall, offers a service for women to have their every need catered to (at a respectful distance). Customers can hire ManServants to refill champagne glasses, hold belongings during errands, or anything else they may need an extra hand for. <strong>Over 35% of content</strong> about ManServants was shared to Twitter.</p>
<h3>Israel and Hamas Declare 12-Hour Gaza Truce</h3>
<p>Israel and the militant group Hamas have been in conflict for about three weeks. The UN asked Israel and Hamas to pause fighting, and the <a title="cease fire lasted twelve hours" href="http://cajavila.com/post/world/israel-and-hamas-declare-12-hour-gaza-truce/" target="_blank">cease-fire lasted twelve hours</a>. Hamas wants to end Israel’s blockade of Gaza’s borders, and Israel wants Hamas to leave Gaza unmilitarized.</p>
