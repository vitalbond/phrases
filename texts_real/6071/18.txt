<h3>Supremes Rule Against Aereo</h3>
<p>Aereo is a technology that allows subscribers to stream live TV through internet-connected devices, potentially transforming the way people watch television. The Supreme Court ruled, however, that <a title="the service is illegal" href="http://www.broadcastingcable.com/news/washington/supremes-rule-against-aereo/132015" target="_blank">the service is illegal</a>––a big win for broadcasters and cable companies. Facebook was the place to talk about the Aereo ruling, making up <strong>37% of content shared</strong> about the topic.</p>
<h3>Supreme Court Bans Warrantless Cell Phone Searches</h3>
<p>In another Supreme Court ruling, it was decided that <a title="cellphones cannot be searched without a warrant" href="http://www.washingtontimes.com/news/2014/jun/25/supreme-court-bans-warrantless-cell-phone-searches/" target="_blank">cellphones cannot be searched without a warrant</a>. Till this point, police have been able to search cell phones their suspects without a warrant. Nowadays, cell phones have evolved to become more than just a phone, and include enough personal information to be considerate private information.</p>
