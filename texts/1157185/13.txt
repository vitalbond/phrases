<p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Spring Chicken &amp; Vegetables Salad</strong></p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><a href="http://www.chicgalleria.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/salad.jpg" rel="shadowbox[sbpost-71667];player=img;"><img class="size-full wp-image-71668 aligncenter" alt="Healthy Spring Recipes" src="http://www.chicgalleria.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/salad.jpg" width="580" height="909"></a></p>
<p>Salads are good for us, but they can be boring to eat. That&rsquo;s why it helps to use several ingredients to make a tasty, textured salad. This spring salad is packed with vegetables, including peas, asparagus and avocados. The wide variety of vegetables mixed with hazelnuts and chicken make a flavorful dish.</p>

<p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Penne with Shrimp, Feta, and Spring Vegetables</strong></p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><a href="http://www.chicgalleria.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/penne.jpg" rel="shadowbox[sbpost-71667];player=img;"><img class="size-full wp-image-71669 aligncenter" alt="Healthy Spring Recipes" src="http://www.chicgalleria.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/penne.jpg" width="580" height="580"></a></p>

<p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Spring Asparagus Risotto</strong></p>
<p><a href="http://www.chicgalleria.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/asparagus-risotto.jpg" rel="shadowbox[sbpost-71667];player=img;"><img class="size-full wp-image-71670 aligncenter" alt="asparagus-risotto" src="http://www.chicgalleria.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/asparagus-risotto.jpg" width="580" height="414"></a></p>
<p>Asparagus tastes the best during the spring months. It&rsquo;s a <a title="Tuesday Entree: Risotto with Asparagus" href="http://www.chicgalleria.com/2012/05/tuesday-entree-risotto-asparagus/">versatile vegetable</a> and tastes great by itself or mixed with pasta, rice or other vegetables. Give this nutritious creamy risotto dish a try.</p>

    </div><p id="zlrecipe-ingredients" class="h-4 strong">Ingredients</p><ul id="zlrecipe-ingredients-list"><li id="zlrecipe-ingredient-0" class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">4 1/2 cups low-sodium chicken or vegetable broth</li><li id="zlrecipe-ingredient-1" class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">2 tsp olive oil</li><li id="zlrecipe-ingredient-2" class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">1/3 cup shallots, chopped</li><li id="zlrecipe-ingredient-3" class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">1 cup arborio rice</li><li id="zlrecipe-ingredient-4" class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">2 oz dry white wine</li><li id="zlrecipe-ingredient-5" class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">3/4 lb thin asparagus spears, tough ends trimmed, cut 2-inches long</li><li id="zlrecipe-ingredient-6" class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">1-2 tsp fresh lemon juice</li><li id="zlrecipe-ingredient-7" class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley</li><li id="zlrecipe-ingredient-8" class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">1/4 cup freshly grated Parmigiano-Reggiano, plus more for serving</li><li id="zlrecipe-ingredient-9" class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">salt and fresh cracked pepper, to taste</li><li id="zlrecipe-ingredient-10" class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">1 tsp grated lemon zest for garnish</li><li id="zlrecipe-ingredient-11" class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients"></ul><p id="zlrecipe-instructions" class="h-4 strong">Instructions</p><ol id="zlrecipe-instructions-list" class="instructions"><li id="zlrecipe-instruction-0" class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">Step 1</li><li id="zlrecipe-instruction-1" class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">In a large saucepan, heat broth over medium-high heat. When it boils, reduce heat to a simmer and maintain over low heat, taste for salt and adjust as needed.</li><li id="zlrecipe-instruction-2" class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">Step 2</li><li id="zlrecipe-instruction-3" class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">In a large heavy saucepan over medium heat, heat the oil and add the shallots; saut&eacute; 3-4 minute. Add the rice; mix well so the rice is coated throughout and saute until the rice is slightly translucent, about 2-3 minutes. Add the wine and stir until it is absorbed. </li><li id="zlrecipe-instruction-4" class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">Step 3</li><li id="zlrecipe-instruction-5" class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">Add a ladleful of the simmering stock; stir and wait until it is absorbed before adding another ladleful stirring gently and almost constantly. </li><li id="zlrecipe-instruction-6" class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">Step 4</li><li id="zlrecipe-instruction-7" class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">Continue this process until the rice is creamy, tender to the bite, but slightly firm in the center. Add the asparagus along with the last ladle of broth and continue cooking 5 minutes, total time should be about 25-30 minutes from the time you started. When all the liquid is absorbed, remove from heat and stir in the lemon juice, parsley and Parmigiano-Reggiano.</li><li id="zlrecipe-instruction-8" class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">Step 5</li><li id="zlrecipe-instruction-9" class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">Serve immediately and top with fresh cracked pepper, lemon zest, and extra grated cheese if desired.</li></ol><div class="zl-linkback">Schema/Recipe SEO Data Markup by <a title="ZipList Recipe Plugin" alt="ZipList Recipe Plugin" href="http://www.ziplist.com/recipe_plugin" target="_blank">ZipList Recipe Plugin</a></div><div class="ziplist-recipe-plugin" style="display: none;">2.2</div><a id="zl-printed-permalink" href="http://www.chicgalleria.com/2014/03/foodie-friday-healthy-spring-recipes/" title="Permalink to Recipe">http://www.chicgalleria.com/2014/03/foodie-friday-healthy-spring-recipes/</a></div></div>
