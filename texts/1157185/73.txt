<div><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Honor, Reflect, and Remember Our Military <span class="zem_slink">Heroes</span></strong></p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><a href="http://www.chicgalleria.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/IMG_2236.jpg" rel="shadowbox[sbpost-73649];player=img;"><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-73650" alt="IMG_2236" src="http://www.chicgalleria.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/IMG_2236.jpg" width="600" height="800"></a><br><strong> The History of <a class="zem_slink" title="Memorial Day" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Memorial_Day" target="_blank" rel="wikipedia">Memorial Day</a></strong></p>
<p>Memorial Day is an <a class="zem_slink" title="Public holidays in the United States" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Public_holidays_in_the_United_States" target="_blank" rel="wikipedia">American holiday</a> that commemorates all those who have died in any war in the nation&rsquo;s service.</p>
<p>Originally called Decoration Day in&nbsp;May 1868 &nbsp;- a day to decorate the graves of the dead with flowers &ndash; and was created three years after the Civil War ended. Established by Maj. Gen. <a class="zem_slink" title="John A. Logan" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_A._Logan" target="_blank" rel="wikipedia">John A. Logan</a>, the date May 30 was chosen and it is believed that date was chosen because flowers would be in bloom all over the country.</p>
<p>Over time, Memorial Day was extended to honor all those who died, in service to the nation, from the Revolutionary War to the present.</p>
