<div><p>Welcome to part 2 of my Healthy Travel Guide! If you missed it, you can go back and read <a title="Healthy Travel Guide: Exercise" href="http://www.chicgalleria.com/2014/06/healthy-travel-guide-exercise/">Healthy Travel Guide: Exercise</a>.</p>
<p>Whether you are traveling to work, another state or a different country, o<span style="line-height: 1.5em;">n-the-go healthy eating</span><span style="line-height: 1.5em;"> is important. Keeping our body fueled for long and grueling days is tough. While it may sound like a daunting task, prepping for healthy travel can be quite quick and painless. Here are seven delicious proposals:</span></p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><a href="http://www.chicgalleria.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/healthy-snacks.jpg" rel="shadowbox[sbpost-73144];player=img;"><img class=" wp-image-73615 aligncenter" alt="healthy travel guide" src="http://www.chicgalleria.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/healthy-snacks.jpg" width="579" height="384"></a></p>
<p><img class="alignleft  wp-image-73687" alt="36388883" src="http://www.chicgalleria.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/36388883.png" width="50" height="50">Water – it is incredibly important to stay hydrated and focus on low-sodium foods. The dry nature of traveling will dehydrate you and puff you up. Either keep a reusable water bottle with you or shuffle out some bucks for water at the airport.</p>
<p> </p>
<p><img class="alignleft size-full wp-image-73686" alt="36388884" src="http://www.chicgalleria.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/36388884.png" width="50" height="50">Fruit! Tip: cut up your own fruit, and pack it in an airtight container. This allows you to control what fruit to bring, how it is washed, and when it is cut up in order to promote the healthiest snack possible.</p>
<p> </p>
<p><img class="alignleft  wp-image-73685" alt="36388887" src="http://www.chicgalleria.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/36388887.png" width="50" height="50">Carrot and celery sticks with <a title="Deliciously Ella's Hummus" href="http://deliciouslyella.com/the-best-hummus/" target="_blank">hummus</a> or <a title="Simple Salsa" href="http://kimberlysnyder.net/blog/2013/06/01/sallys-salsa/" target="_blank">simple salsa</a> are great savory snacks that aren’t loaded with sodium. Snacks with high sodium can be detrimental while traveling, making you puffy and even more dehydrated.</p>
<p> </p>
<p><img class="alignleft  wp-image-73684" alt="36388890" src="http://www.chicgalleria.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/36388890.png" width="50" height="50">Raw Granola is a great crunchy treat that you can either buy (at a local Whole Foods) or <a title="Raw Granola Recipe" href="http://thehealthyeatingsite.com/best-raw-granola/" target="_blank">make</a> (if you have a dehydrator).</p>
<p> </p>
<p><img class="alignleft size-full wp-image-73683" alt="36388891" src="http://www.chicgalleria.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/36388891.png" width="50" height="50">Organic Dark Chocolate – for those of you that have a sweet tooth.<a href="http://www.chicgalleria.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/healthy-snacks.jpg" rel="shadowbox[sbpost-73144];player=img;"><br></a></p>
<p> </p>
<p><em><a title="Sensibliss Balls" href="http://www.movenourishbelieve.com/nourish/your-go-to-guide-to-healthy-snacking-6-smart-snack-ideas/" target="_blank"><img class="alignleft  wp-image-73682" alt="36388892" src="http://www.chicgalleria.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/36388892.png" width="50" height="50">Bliss Balls</a> </em>like these made by Move.Nourish.Believe are filled with delicious and nutritious ingredients to give us energy and keep us going.</p>
<p><a title="Raw Energy Bars" href="http://www.rawmazing.com/cacao-dried-cherry-energy-bars/" target="_blank"><img class="alignleft size-full wp-image-73681" alt="36388895" src="http://www.chicgalleria.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/36388895.png" width="50" height="50">Raw Energy Bars</a>, wonderfully healthy snacks, fuel your body with natural plant-based ingredients.</p>
