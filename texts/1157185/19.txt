<div><p>The third Vogue Festival will take place on Saturday 29 and Sunday 30 March 2014, this year in partnership with Harrods. The two-day extravaganza promises to be even bigger than its two predecessors, and will be held at the Queen Elizabeth Hall at the Southbank Centre.</p>
<p><img class="aligncenter  wp-image-72793" alt="VogueFestival-Collage-final2-vogue-rasha-17feb14_b_1440x960_1" src="http://www.chicgalleria.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/VogueFestival-Collage-final2-vogue-rasha-17feb14_b_1440x960_1-1024x682.jpg" width="580" height="386"></p>
<p>&ldquo;I&rsquo;m delighted that Harrods is going to be our partner for the Vogue Festival 2014,&rdquo; said Vogue editor Alexandra Shulman. &ldquo;We&rsquo;ve already been discussing some great new ideas, and with their well-known commitment to fashion and style, I&rsquo;m looking forward to a terrific relationship.&rdquo;</p>
<p>Last year&rsquo;s festival saw some of the world&rsquo;s biggest fashion talents come together for a weekend of interviews and discussions, including Victoria Beckham, Donatella Versace, Michael Kors, Paul Smith, Mario Testino, Vivienne Westwood, Alber Elbaz, Alexa Chung and Cara Delevingne. The line-up for the 2014 event is yet to be announced, but expect plenty of entertaining and inspiring star guests, as well as workshops, performances and makeovers. Tickets will go on sale in mid-February.</p>
<p>&ldquo;We are delighted to be partnering with Vogue for the Vogue Festival 2014,&rdquo; said Chiara Varese, Harrods director of customer relationship management and personal shopping. &ldquo;The fashion world is artistic, inspirational, innovative and sophisticated, but most of all it&rsquo;s exciting and fun. We look forward to adding to the unique customer experience for which this event has become well known and best loved. This natural partnership between our two brands will bring together in an innovative way the world&rsquo;s leading influencers, designers and fashion-savvy crowd, in luxury style.&rdquo;</p>
<p>For all the latest updates and action, follow&nbsp;<a href="https://twitter.com/BritishVogue" target="_blank">@BritishVogue</a>&nbsp;on Twitter and use the&nbsp;<a href="https://twitter.com/search?q=%23VogueFestival&amp;src=typd" target="_blank">#VogueFestival</a>&nbsp;hashtag to take part in the festival fun.</p>
<p><strong>Personal Style And Do You Have It?</strong></p>
<p>What is great style? Is it something that you can learn or are you are simply born with it? Lucinda Chambers, Alexa Chung and Amanda Harlech &ndash; three women renowned throughout the fashion industry for their impeccable personal style &ndash; debate its definition, its magic and mystery, and reveal how they put their own looks together. A must-see for all style-seekers and fashion queens.</p>
<p><strong>The Power Of Two In Fashion</strong></p>
<p>In the whirlwind world of fashion design, are two heads now better than one? From starting out as a twosome to building a brand with a single vision, New York&rsquo;s leading fashion duo, Jack McCollough and Lazaro Hernandez of Proenza Schouler discuss the pros and cons of doubling up. With long-term collaborators and arbiters of London cool Luella Bartley and Katie Hillier.</p>
<p><strong>The Philo Factor: Reinventing Cool</strong></p>
<p>The fashion designer renowned for her instantly recognisable aesthetic and personal brand of laid-back chic talks to Alexandra Shulman about her career, the philosophy that guided her, and how she interpreted C&eacute;line, turning it into one of the most influential brands of our time.</p>
<p><strong>My Fashion Life</strong></p>
<p>Legendary shoe designer Manolo Blahnik, US womenswear mogul Tory Burch and Angela Missoni, creative director of a fashion dynasty, reveal the story of their lives and careers.</p>
<p><strong>Valentino: King Of Glamour</strong></p>
<p>Valentino Garavani talks to Alexandra Shulman about how he built a business and lifestyle that became one of the most envied in fashion.</p>
<p><strong>Fashion, Beauty And The Digital Revolution</strong></p>
<p>In a digital world changing at lightning speed, what&rsquo;s next for the industry? Join digital photography pioneer Nick Knight; fashion designer Mary Katrantzou; Pixie Geldof of Funky Offish; and digital investor and entrepreneur Miroslava Duma to learn what they&rsquo;ve discovered and what the future holds.</p>
<p><strong>Good Taste, Bad Taste &ndash; The Shock Of The New</strong></p>
<p>Join Turner Prize-winning artist Grayson Perry, singer and songwriter Lily Allen and British designer Jasper Conran in a conversation about what taste is &shy;- and does it matter?</p>
<p><strong>Want To Work In Fashion? Vogue&rsquo;s Fashion Masterclass</strong></p>
<p>Lisa Armstrong, fashion editor of <em>The Telegraph</em>; Sarah Burton, creative director at Alexander McQueen; Daniel Marks, director of The Communications Store; Alexandra Shulman, editor of <em>Vogue</em>; and Louise Wilson, course director at Central Saint Martins, offer personal insights in how to break into their industries. Don&rsquo;t miss this once-in-a-lifetime opportunity to get advice from the experts on how to follow in their footsteps &ndash; and ask the question that could kick-start your career.</p>
<p><strong>Fashion Legends</strong></p>
<p>Editor of Italian <em>Vogue</em>, UN ambassador and fashion visionary Franca Sozzani and supermodel and television presenter Naomi Campbell talk about how they built their careers, why Africa could be the future of luxury, and the issue of diversity in fashion.</p>
