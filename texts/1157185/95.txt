<div><p style="text-align: center;">Spring <a class="zem_slink" title="BBQ Chicken Wrap" href="http://www.mccormick.com/Recipes/Main-Dishes/Slow-Cookers-Buffalo-BBQ-Chicken-Lettuce-Wraps" target="_blank" rel="mccormick">Chicken</a> with Vegetable Couscous &#38; Pesto</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><img class="aligncenter  wp-image-73773" alt="Vegetable Couscous" src="http://www.chicgalleria.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/12.jpg" width="580" height="387"></p>
<p>Spring Chicken is the hallmark dish of the season. For our Mother’s Day dinner this year, I simply roasted the succulent chicken legs and served them with vegetable <a class="zem_slink" title="Couscous" href="http://www.williams-sonoma.com/recipe/moroccan-couscous-salad.html" target="_blank" rel="williamssonoma">couscous</a> and a dollop of pesto on top.</p>
<p>Couscous is the Middle Eastern pasta made from wheat dough rolled into tiny pearls. It is very versatile and works best when mixed with finely chopped ingredients like olives, cucumbers, and even dried fruit. Bell peppers, radishes and sweet green peas were my choice of vegetables for the couscous this time. Their succulent crunch is really satisfying! Tangy, lemony dressing infuses the pasta and vegetables in the most delectable way. Serve the chicken with a spoonful of vegetable couscous.</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><img class="aligncenter  wp-image-73774" alt="Vegetable Couscous" src="http://www.chicgalleria.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/21.jpg" width="579" height="386"></p>
<p>Ingredients: chicken legs, baby arugula, garlic cloves, walnuts, Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese, extra-virgin olive oil, lemon, Israeli couscous, bell peppers, green peas, radishes, romaine lettuce, parsley, white wine vinegar, salt and freshly ground black pepper.</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><img class="aligncenter  wp-image-73775" alt="Vegetable Couscous" src="http://www.chicgalleria.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/31.jpg" width="580" height="395"></p>
<p>Make the dressing: In a small blender, whiz together parsley, garlic, lemon zest, vinegar and olive oil. Season with salt and pepper. Taste the dressing, and adjust the seasoning.</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-73777" alt="Vegetable Couscous" src="http://www.chicgalleria.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/blend.jpg" width="580" height="606"></p>
<p>Make the couscous: Cook the pasta according to the instructions on the packet, then drain and toss with the dressing. Add chopped bell peppers, green peas and radishes. Toss to combine.</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><img class="aligncenter  wp-image-73776" alt="Vegetable Couscous" src="http://www.chicgalleria.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/61.jpg" width="580" height="869"></p>
<p>Preheat the oven to 400 F. Drizzle the chicken with olive oil, season generously with kosher salt, Aleppo peppers and freshly ground black pepper.</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-73782" alt="Vegetable Couscous" src="http://www.chicgalleria.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/mix.jpg" width="580" height="606"></p>
<p>Heat a large frying pan over medium-high heat, and cook the chicken skin side down for 3-4 minutes until nicely golden brown.</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><img class="aligncenter  wp-image-73778" alt="Vegetable Couscous" src="http://www.chicgalleria.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/91.jpg" width="580" height="870"></p>
<p>Transfer the chicken to a roasting dish, and bake for 30 minutes or until the juices run clear when pierced with the knife (the internal temperature should be 165 F).</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><img class="aligncenter  wp-image-73779" alt="Vegetable Couscous" src="http://www.chicgalleria.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/101.jpg" width="580" height="871"></p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><img class="aligncenter  wp-image-73780" alt="Vegetable Couscous" src="http://www.chicgalleria.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/111.jpg" width="580" height="871"></p>
<p>To serve, spoon the vegetable couscous on each plate, place the chicken on top, and finish with a dollop of arugula pesto. Place few baby romaine lettuce leaves on each plate as a garnish.</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><img class="aligncenter  wp-image-73781" alt="Vegetable Couscous" src="http://www.chicgalleria.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/121.jpg" width="579" height="869"></p>
