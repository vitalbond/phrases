<div><p>In the 18th and 19th century people who wished to be regarded as true sophisticates ensured they visited the magnificent treasures Italy had given to the world. Florence city breaks are exceptional choices for those who want to <a href="http://www.expedia.co.uk/Florence.d179893.Holidays-City-Breaks" target="_blank">experience the finest historical wonders</a> that Italy has to offer. The Tuscany region as a whole is a treasure trove not to be missed.</p>
<p><img class="aligncenter  wp-image-73371" alt="Cathedral-Of-Santa-Maria-Del-Fiore-Duomo-26725" src="http://www.chicgalleria.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Cathedral-Of-Santa-Maria-Del-Fiore-Duomo-26725.jpg" width="579" height="325"></p>
<p>Home to more palaces, art collections, mansions, piazzas and ancient bridges than all of Britain&rsquo;s cities put together, a Florence city break tour presents visitors with a window in time, where travelers are allowed to catch a glimpse of how the modern world was created.</p>
<p><b>UNESCO World Heritage</b></p>
<p>Sip a steaming cup of espresso at the Caff&egrave; Guibbe Rosse overlooking the magnificent <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Piazza_della_Repubblica,_Florence" target="_blank">Piazza della Repubblica</a> and imagine you are surrounded by some of the 20th century&rsquo;s most influential artists and writers &ndash; it was one of their favourite haunts in Florence and ranks as one of the most important literati coffeehouses in Europe.</p>
<p>Stroll across the Piazza Santa Croce and marvel at the gorgeous Bascilica or be utterly moved by Michelangelo&rsquo;s &ldquo;David&rdquo; at the Galleria dell&rsquo; Accademia, where many of his masterpieces are displayed.</p>
<p>When you stand on the 14th century Ponte Vecchio that spans the River Arno you can picture what it must have been like for nobles from the de Medici family to stride confidently across to their magnificent Palazzo Pitti by Boboli&rsquo;s Gardens, having just caused political mayhem at the Uffizi.</p>
<p>Built for the Grand Duke Francisco de Medici in 1581, the Uffizi originally housed offices for Florence&rsquo;s administrators, but is now home to one of the world&rsquo;s most important art collections.</p>
<p><b>Tuscan treasures</b></p>
<p>Visit the Villa Medici, the Etruscan walled fortifications circling parts of Fiesole or the gorgeous 11th century Cathedral of St. Romulus. If you go to Fiesole by car, make time for the Vallombrosa Monastery where you&rsquo;ll finally shake off Florence&rsquo;s tourist hordes and meet the real Tuscany.</p>
<p><b>Conclusion</b></p>
<p>Florence is utterly bewitching and you&rsquo;ll certainly want to return to this romantic city again and again. Take note that you&rsquo;ll only appreciate its beauty if you&rsquo;ll sign up for a city break tour.</p>
