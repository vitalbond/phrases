<div><p><em>Oven-Baked Sea Bream with Lemon &amp; Oregano</em> is an impressive delicate entr&eacute;e, which at the same time is elegantly simple. Start with the freshest fish possible. If you live in New York City, Citarella would be your best bet; it has a superb selection of fish and seafood that&rsquo;s always fresh.</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-73000" alt="Oven-Baked Sea Bream with Lemon" src="http://www.chicgalleria.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/fish.jpg" width="580" height="461"></p>
<p>Handpick two whole Sea Bream or Branzino fish, approximately half a pound each. They should smell ocean-y, not fishy, and have nice shiny scales with clear eyes. Then ask the fishmonger to fillet them for you. Do not overpower with herbs; this fish requires three humble ingredients &ndash; lemon, good quality sweet and fruity olive oil, and fresh oregano leaves! Bake the fillets for only ten minutes to ensure that they will be moist. I guarantee that the buttery, tender Sea Bream will surely please your guests! Serve along with <a href="http://artdefete.com/persimmon-orange-pomegranate-salad/">Persimmon, Blood Orange &amp; Pomegranate Salad</a>, which is in itself quite excellent with all those winter fruits. A chilled flowery Chardonnay from California will nicely complement this dish.</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><img class="aligncenter  wp-image-73001" alt="Oven-Baked Sea Bream with Lemon" src="http://www.chicgalleria.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/anyafoto_art_de_fete_12-2_01311.jpg" width="579" height="870"></p>
<p>Fresh oregano has bright green, velvety leaves, which should be free of any browning or mold. This aromatic, slightly spicy herb is predominately used in Mediterranean cooking and works wonderfully with the fish.</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><img class="aligncenter  wp-image-73002" alt="Oven-Baked Sea Bream with Lemon" src="http://www.chicgalleria.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/anyafoto_art_de_fete_12-2_0008.jpg" width="579" height="385"></p>
<p>Preheat the oven to 425 degrees F. Using mandoline or a very sharp knife, slice the lemon. Drizzle baking sheet with olive oil, and lay down lemon slices in four rows, 3-4 pieces for each fillet.</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-73003" alt="Oven-Baked Sea Bream with Lemon" src="http://www.chicgalleria.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/fish-2.jpg" width="580" height="461"></p>
<p>Place sea bream fillet skin side up on the work surface. Using the tip of a sharp knife, make several shallow slashes in the skin about 1 inch apart.&nbsp;Place the fillets on top of lemon slices and drizzle with olive oil. Season generously with salt and freshly ground black pepper.</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><img class="aligncenter  wp-image-73004" alt="Oven-Baked Sea Bream with Lemon" src="http://www.chicgalleria.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/anyafoto_art_de_fete_12-2_0019.jpg" width="579" height="870"></p>
<p>Scatter oregano leaves on top of sea bream fillets, and bake for 10-12 minutes, depending on the thickness.</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><img class="aligncenter  wp-image-73007" alt="Oven-Baked Sea Bream with Lemon" src="http://www.chicgalleria.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/anyafoto_art_de_fete_12-2_0188.jpg" width="579" height="870"></p>
<p>Serve along with <a href="http://artdefete.com/persimmon-orange-pomegranate-salad/">Persimmon, Blood Orange &amp; Pomegranate Salad</a>.</p>
