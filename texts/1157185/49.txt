<div><p>Tourists often flock to the White House, the Washington Memorial and maybe even the Spy Museum, but what I enjoy most in D.C. is its rich collection of art. If you&rsquo;re not much of an art scholar, the majority of the museums are free, so it won&rsquo;t cost you anything to take a minute (or a day) and admire the visual arts that D.C. has to offer!</p>
<p><strong>The National Gallery of Art</strong></p>
<p><a href="http://www.nga.gov/content/ngaweb.html">The National Gallery </a>should be the first stop on your list of museums to visit. It holds the most comprehensive collection as you&rsquo;ll find a mixture of works from the 13th century to present day (think of it as your Art History: 101 class in college). It&rsquo;s a great way to survey different time periods, art movements, etc.</p>

<p style="text-align: center;"><a href="http://www.chicgalleria.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/photo.PNG-e1396721990804.jpeg" rel="shadowbox[sbpost-72904];player=img;"><img class="aligncenter  wp-image-72932" alt="Art" src="http://www.chicgalleria.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/photo.PNG-e1396721990804.jpeg" width="521" height="522"></a></p>
<p><strong>The Phillips Collection</strong></p>
<p>This is a more intimate gallery; as its permanent collection is located in a renovated version of Duncan Phillips&rsquo; house in Dupont Circle. It boasts a large range of work centered around Impressionism/Modernism with artists&rsquo; like Matisse, Monet and Picasso. The Phillips also holds various Artist lectures and educational programs.</p>
<p><strong>The Hirshhorn Museum and Sculpture Garden</strong></p>
<p>I saved my personal favorite for last.&nbsp;The <a href="http://www.hirshhorn.si.edu/collection/home/">Hirshhorn</a> is where you&rsquo;ll find the best collection of contemporary art. &nbsp;It&rsquo;s not uncommon for artists&rsquo; work like Ai Wei Wei and <a href="http://www.hirshhorn.si.edu/collection/home/#collection=barbara-kruger">Barbara Kruger</a> to take up a majority of the gallery with their body of work. As someone drawn to bold colors and abstraction, pieces like Sam Francis&rsquo; <a href="http://www.hirshhorn.si.edu/collection/home/#collection=gravitys-edge&amp;detail=http%3A//www.hirshhorn.si.edu/search-results/search-result-details/%3Fedan_search_value%3Dhmsg_86.1870">&ldquo;Untitled (Mako&rsquo;s Rain)&rdquo;</a> are right up my alley of interests.</p>
