<div><p>While it may be true that diamonds are a girl&rsquo;s best friend, choosing the right one isn&rsquo;t always an easy task. To help make choosing the perfect diamond a bit less confusing, <b>Andrew Fox, CEO of leading online jewelry purveyor,&nbsp;</b><b><a href="http://www.superjeweler.com/" target="_blank">SuperJeweler.com</a>,</b> has broken down the process to what is commonly known as the <b>&ldquo;Five C&rsquo;s.&rdquo;</b></p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-73332" alt="Diamonds" src="http://www.chicgalleria.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Andrew-Fox.jpg" width="580" height="580"></p>
<p><b><span style="text-decoration: underline;">Color:</span></b></p>
<p>Although Diamonds occur naturally in a variety of colors, the average person thinks of Diamonds as white or colorless. Color in Diamond grading actually refers to subtle differences in the body color or hue. The <a class="zem_slink" title="Diamond color" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diamond_color" target="_blank" rel="wikipedia">Diamond color</a> scale begins at &ldquo;D&rdquo; (colorless) and goes up to &ldquo;Z&rdquo; (intense yellow). Actual colorless Diamonds (D &ndash; G) are quite expensive. At the cusp of the color scale are &ldquo;L &ndash; M,&rdquo; since most consumers notice color in this range. Lower than &ldquo;M&rdquo; is very easy for consumers to see color.</p>
<p><b><span style="text-decoration: underline;">Clarity:22</span></b></p>
<p>Clarity refers to the presence (or lack thereof) distinguishing characteristics on/within the stone and is an important factor to determine a Diamond&rsquo;s value. The clarity of the Diamonds are graded based on an appearance under 10x magnification to determine its internal features within the stone (called inclusions) as well as its surface irregularities (called blemishes). The scale ranges from flawless (FL), which means no inclusions or blemishes are visible under magnification, to I1 through I3, which means inclusions are obvious under magnification and likely to affect the transparency and brilliance of the stone.</p>
<p><b><span style="text-decoration: underline;">Cut</span></b></p>
<p>Many people think a diamond&rsquo;s cut refers to its shape (e.g., round, oval, etc.). Cut actually concerns the proportions, symmetry and reflective qualities of a diamond. The Cut of a Diamond is very important as it affects a Diamond&rsquo;s beauty and value. A finer cut gives a Diamond more brilliance, which to the consumer is what a Diamond is really all about. When we refer to the cut of a Diamond, we are really referring to a compilation of two factors &ndash; its proportions in terms of angles, degrees and percentages, and the qualities of its finish.</p>
<p><b><span style="text-decoration: underline;">Carat Weight</span></b></p>
<p>The Carat indicates the size of the diamond. One carat is 200 milligrams, 5 carats equals 1.00 gram. One carat is divided into 100 parts (called &ldquo;points&rdquo;). Therefore, a 25-point Diamond weighs &frac14; or 0.25 of a carat, and a 50-point Diamond weighs &frac12; or 0.50 ct. etc. The more carats a diamond has, the rarer and typically more expensive the diamond.</p>
<p><b><span style="text-decoration: underline;">Certification (The Unspoken Fifth C)</span></b></p>
<p>The fifth C stands for Certification, which protects your investment. Whoever you are working with should instill a feeling of trust and confidence in you. Any diamond you are considering should be accompanied by a diamond certificate or grading report. This is a quality certification issued by an independent gem laboratory, and it verifies the authenticity and characteristics (the four C&rsquo;s above) of the stone.</p>
