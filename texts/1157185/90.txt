<div><p style="text-align: center;">Sponge Cake with <a class="zem_slink" title="Cream" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cream" target="_blank" rel="wikipedia">Cream</a>, Fruit &#38; Berries</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><img class="aligncenter  wp-image-73717" alt="Sponge Cake" src="http://www.chicgalleria.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/1.jpg" width="580" height="869"></p>
<p>The basic sponge cake gets exotic with fresh berries, succulent figs and baby kiwis. Bake the sponges to a light golden perfection, poke them with skewers and spoon some simple vanilla syrup on top to keep them moist and sweet. The whipped cream filling adds a lavish touch to this cake’s heavenly texture. The end result will be a delightful, delicious and gorgeous dessert that your guests will devour. Mine sure did!</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><img class="aligncenter  wp-image-73718" alt="Sponge Cake" src="http://www.chicgalleria.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/2.jpg" width="579" height="386"></p>
<p>Place the butter and sugar into a large mixing bowl and beat with a hand-mixer until creamy and pale.</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><img class="aligncenter  wp-image-73719" alt="Sponge Cake" src="http://www.chicgalleria.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/3.jpg" width="579" height="386"></p>
<p>Add the eggs and vanilla to the bowl, and mix to combine. Then sift the flour, baking powder and salt on top. With a large, metal spoon, in a figure 8 motion, incorporate dry ingredients into the batter. Mix the batter until even and smooth; try not to overwork it.  Add a dash of milk if it looks too dry.</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><img class="aligncenter  wp-image-73720" alt="Sponge Cake" src="http://www.chicgalleria.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/4.jpg" width="580" height="870"></p>
<p>Divide the batter between the pans and gently smooth the tops. Bake for 25-30 minutes or until the sponges are evenly golden and come away from the sides of the baking pans. Let the cakes cool in their pans for 15 minutes; turn them onto the wire rack. Peel and discard the parchment paper.</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><img class="aligncenter  wp-image-73721" alt="Sponge Cake" src="http://www.chicgalleria.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/5.jpg" width="580" height="869"></p>
<p>In a small pan, add 2 tablespoons of water and 2 tablespoons of sugar. Heat gently until the sugar dissolves. Remove from heat, add ½ teaspoon vanilla extract and let cool. Make the holes all over the sponges with tooth picks.</p>
<p>Then spoon over the cooled syrup.</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><img class="aligncenter  wp-image-73723" alt="Sponge Cake" src="http://www.chicgalleria.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/7.jpg" width="580" height="869"></p>
<p>Cut strawberries, blueberries, figs and baby kiwis in half.</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><img class="aligncenter  wp-image-73724" alt="Sponge Cake" src="http://www.chicgalleria.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/8.jpg" width="580" height="869"></p>
<p>Pour the cream into a large bowl; add sugar and vanilla. Whip until thickened but not stiff. Spread one cake with the cream mixture.</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><img class="aligncenter  wp-image-73725" alt="Sponge Cake" src="http://www.chicgalleria.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/9.jpg" width="580" height="870"></p>
<p>Top with the remaining cake. Dust the cake with confectioners’ sugar, and garnish with fruit and berries.</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-73727" alt="Sponge Cake" src="http://www.chicgalleria.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/10.jpg" width="580" height="870"></p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><img class="aligncenter  wp-image-73726" alt="Sponge Cake" src="http://www.chicgalleria.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/11.jpg" width="580" height="869"></p>
