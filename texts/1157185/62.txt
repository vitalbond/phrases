<p>While visiting Arizona, my girlfriend invited me to see the inspiring documentary, &ldquo;Walking the Camino: Six Ways to Santiago.&rdquo; The documentary follows six strangers from diverse walks of life walking the 500-mile pilgrimage across Spain, known as The Camino de Santiago. I enjoyed this film so much.</p>
<p>April 2013, Kathy (my girlfriend) decided to undertake this inspiring journey and walked the Camino. I am in AWE of her courage, strength and bravery. Bravo!</p>
<p>Learning&nbsp;journeys&nbsp;are unique collections of travel experiences and are necessary&nbsp;when we go through <a title="Transitions In Life" href="http://www.chicgalleria.com/2009/03/transitions-in-life/">transitions in life</a> &ndash; it holds within new beginnings. There is peace to be found in knowing&nbsp;&nbsp;you will thrive&nbsp;during&nbsp;times&nbsp;of transition&hellip; we open our awareness to&nbsp;new&nbsp;perceptions and&nbsp;new&nbsp;perspectives.</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><img class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-72545" alt="Walking the Camino" src="http://www.chicgalleria.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/3c784166df93668902a69f3221faefca-467x1024.jpg" width="467" height="1024"></p>

<p>Synopsis</p>
<p>&ldquo;500 miles on foot. Bunk-beds. Blisters. Stunning landscapes. World-class snorers. Hot searing sun, freezing cold rain. Kindness from strangers. Debilitating injury. Unexpected romance. No toilet paper when you really need it. Profound grief and deep doubt. Hunger. Laughing with new friends. Total exhaustion. You are guaranteed to experience all of this when walking the ancient pilgrim path, the <a href="http://caminodocumentary.org/the_camino_de_santiago.php">Camino de Santiago</a>.&rdquo;</p>
