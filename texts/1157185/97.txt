<div><p><strong>Hola from San Jose Cabo, <a class="zem_slink" title="Mexico" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mexico" target="_blank" rel="wikipedia">Mexico</a></strong></p>
<p>Being married to an Englishman, I expected the next few weeks to hold long moments of rearranged priorities and the occasional distant gaze as my husband comes to terms with each and every life altering result from <a class="zem_slink" title="2014 FIFA World Cup" href="http://www.fifa.com/worldcup/index.html" target="_blank" rel="homepage">Brazil 2014</a>. And I wasn’t disappointed. Sure enough, our day to day lives took on minor adjustments. There was his sudden need to work nights so that the mornings were free — the need to cancel episodes of <em>Chopped</em> and <em>Big Bang Theory</em> in favor of a soccer game between several countries that I had to look up on the map (Where exactly is Ivory Coast? Cameroon?).</p>
<p>Today was no different as I was (literally) dragged from our delightful hotel pool to a bar in Cabo showing England vs. Uruguay. But guess what? Gradually, I start to get into it. I know – I’m as surprised as you. Through a process of Osmosis, suddenly, it all clicked! OMG! Spain Eliminated?! What about Japan? USA? Whoa! What next?</p>
<p>Today, I finally became a <strong>raging</strong> fan by default after England equalized in today’s group game in the 74:00 min. I hardly recognized myself… screaming like a lunatic at the tele! But England’s charitable defending… haunted by “the king of Uruguay” ( Louis Suarez) eventually resulted in a horrific defeat. I was mortified. My husband was thankful it was Happy Hour and counted the four years until his next hope of redemption.</p>
<p>England now needs a miracle to get out of their group. But this former “Widow” will be praying with the converted!</p>
<p>Come on England!</p>

<p style="text-align: left;">This fairy tale is not a happy ending… Another four years… “I will be 57 years old, I’m running out of time!” Last World Cup for England was 1966. “I am happy it was Happy Hour. It’s the only thing that went right today!”</p>
