<p style="text-align: left;">For the past few years, skinny jeans have been <em>the</em> trend; I mean why else have we all become obsessed with working out? Well ladies, the tides have turned. It is finally time for comfort and style to rise together. Boyfriend jeans are the ideal addition to your closet.</p>
<p>They are diverse; whether you are edgy, tomboy, classic, modern, or feminine, you can work boyfriend jeans into your outfit rotation. While you may think that baggy bottoms call for a fitting top, you will be surprised to find out that layers work wonderfully. Just check out the three beautiful bloggers above and how they uniquely layer their boyfriend jeans.</p>
<p><em>From left to right:</em></p>
<p><a title="No Boys Allowed" href="http://www.loveblair.com/2014/03/no-boys-allowed.html" target="_blank">Love, Blair</a> pairs her light distressed denim with a loose fitting dark tee and long cardigan. Next, <a title="Day in the Life // L.A." href="http://sincerelyjules.com/2013/09/day-in-the-life-l-a.html" target="_blank">Sincerely Jules</a>&nbsp;highlights a casual and bohemian style with a bright patterned jacket, strappy sandals, and a tee. Katie Cassidy of <a title="Your Boyfriends Jeans" href="http://tomboykc.com/what-katie-wore-your-boyfriends-jeans/" target="_blank">Tomboy KC</a>&nbsp;went more preppy with a collared shirt and sweater with black and white pumps. Finally, here&rsquo;s my personal take on boyfriend jeans (which also can be found <a title="Brunch with the Boyfriend Jeans" href="http://knottobsessed.com/2014/02/14/brunch-with-the-boyfriend-jeans/" target="_blank">here</a>), where I mix a white tee, cream sweater, and a large scarf.</p>
