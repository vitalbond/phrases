<p style="text-align: center;"><em>Accessories</em></p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><img class="aligncenter" style="border: 0px none;" title="Hot Yoga Accessories" alt="Hot Yoga Accessories" src="http://cfc.polyvoreimg.com/cgi/img-set/.sig/2Sc7P1Fo3Qrz7YDSn1G3kw/cid/109934339/id/6PZX4dx64xGu8X_VlJXARQ/size/c600x463.jpg" width="600" height="463" border="0"></p>
<p>While accessories are usually comprised of hats, necklaces, sunglasses, shoes, and more, to a chic fitness-goer it holds a different meaning. Although they are still items that can support, make, or break a girl&rsquo;s style, they are also incredibly essential. If you come to a hot yoga class without these accessories, you may be outed or labeled as a &ldquo;newbie&rdquo; (although many yoga studios have the supplies to help you out). Yet, if you choose these five must-have items wisely, you&rsquo;ll come across as not only prepared, but stylish.</p>
<p><em>Must-have Accessories:</em></p>
<p><em></em><strong>1. Yoga mat</strong><br>
The basic and most obvious accessory. Due to yoga&rsquo;s popularity there is a plethora of choices in this department. From <a href="http://bit.ly/1iXl8PS" target="_blank">Target</a> to <a href="http://bit.ly/1dRxYuv" target="_blank">Victoria&rsquo;s Secret</a>, Lulu Lemon to <a href="http://bit.ly/1eE5DJD" target="_blank">Alexander Wang</a>, you can find any color and any price point that fits your style and budget.</p>
<p><strong>2. Yoga carrier</strong><br>
Instead of inconveniently carrying around a mat in your hand, there are plenty of options that allow the quick transport of your mat. Beginning with the simplicity of a yoga strap, which merely holds your mat together and provides a shoulder strap (simple and great for on the go). Next step would be a yoga bag (featured above). Finally, there are gym bags that are wonderful for accommodating yoga mats.</p>
<p><strong>3. Yoga skidless towel</strong><br>
One important factor that many people don&rsquo;t consider is sweat. While your body is dripping from every pore things on a bare mat can get a little slippery. The handy skidless towel that fits your yoga mat will keep you and the mat from unnecessary movement &ndash; balancing in Eagle Pose is tough enough.</p>
<p><strong>4. Water bottle</strong><br>
As you are sweating buckets (and more), you need to intake water to keep from passing out. Hydrating before, during, and after exercise (especially one as demanding as hot yoga) is extremely important. Thankfully water bottles are even more customizable to your style than a yoga mat.</p>
<p><strong>5. Hand towel</strong><br>
Last, but not least, an accessory to help your face and, more importantly, your vision. In order to balance and work on your form (in a mirror filled room), you have to keep sweat from dripping in your eyes; this hand towel will be a savior.</p>
