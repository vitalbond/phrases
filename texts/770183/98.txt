<div><p><strong>Tiltor</strong>:</p>
<p>Tiltor is a new startup that aims to influence crowd behavior at any place so desired results comes out.</p>
<p>They are using reward system to affect the collective behavior of an assembly or crowd.</p>
<p>By using location-based group rewards, Tiltor can incentivize portions of a crowd to favor one result over another.</p>
<p>These people act to bring about the desired result and, in so doing, influence the rest of the crowd to follow suit.</p>
<p><a href="http://visionwiz.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/crowd-behavior.png"><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-35666" title="crowd behavior" src="http://visionwiz.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/crowd-behavior.png" alt="" width="167" height="88"></a></p>
<p><strong>How it works</strong><strong></strong></p>
<p>First interested parties sponsor a group reward for a designated area (“The crowd gives a standing ovation”)</p>
<p>The reward is advertised in that area and anyone there with a smartphone may sign up for it on Tiltor.</p>
<p>If the desired group behavior is achieved (there is a standing ovation), each signed up person shares a portion of the total prize.</p>
<p>So with very little setup, and no oversight, the desired group behavior is achieved. If it isn’t, the sponsor is returned their money.</p>
