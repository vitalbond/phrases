<div><p><strong>5 Startups From  TechStar’s London Demo Days</strong></p>
<p>1.</p>
<p><a href="http://spatch.co/">Spatch</a></p>
<p>Spatch is  a new smart email organizing  startup that aims to  integrate with your favorite productivity apps.</p>
<p>This startup will help you to sync meetings to your calendar, details to your contact list and tasks to your To Do lists.</p>
<p>Spatch also highlight key phrases, questions and sentences. From there you can interact and turn it into structure.</p>
<p>2.</p>
<p><a href="http://www.eatinmyseat.com/">Shortcut</a></p>
<p>Shortcut is a  startup company  that aims to help you order food  and other items from your seat at any events.</p>
<p>3.</p>
<p><a href="http://www.tapingo.com/">Tapingo</a></p>
<p>Tapingo aims to help students shop for dinning on and around college campuses through their smartphone.</p>

<p><strong>4.</strong></p>
<p><strong><a href="http://lingvist.io/">Lingvist</a></strong></p>
<p>Lingvist is a language learning tool that uses mathematical optimisation  tool  to tailors tasks according to your knowledge and skills.</p>
<p>5.</p>
<p><strong><a href="http://www.pubble.io/questions">Pubble</a></strong></p>
<p>Pubble  is a  new Q&#38;A based startup that aims to help you to engage with your community right on top of the pages that their questions relate to. This helps you to deliver better answers and improves your pages for the next visitor.</p>
