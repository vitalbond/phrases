<div><p><a href="http://www.iposture.com/">iPosture is an electronic device that aims to improve posture</a>. iPosture automatically senses when the body slouches, and it alerts the user with brief vibrations to correct it.</p>
<p><strong>i<a href="http://www.iposture.com/">Posture</a>:</strong></p>
<p><a href="http://www.iposture.com/">iPosture measures the angle of your upper body.</a>&nbsp;When y<span style="line-height: 1.5em;">ou set the iPosture, it remembers the angle of your body in the correct posture.</span></p>
<p>As you slouch, the iPosture can detecet even the smallest change in angle and alert you to<a href="http://visionwiz.net/2011/08/new-webcam-tool-to-improve-posture-of-office-workers/">&nbsp;correct your posture.</a></p>
<p><a href="http://visionwiz.net/wp-content/uploads/2011/09/iposture.png"><img title="iposture" alt="" src="http://visionwiz.net/wp-content/uploads/2011/09/iposture.png" width="161" height="25"></a>Once you set the iPosture, it will remember your correct posture. If you slouch for more than 60 seconds, the iPosture will alert you with a brief vibration.</p>
<p>Once you&nbsp;<a href="http://visionwiz.net/2010/12/worlds-first-ergonomic-excercise-chair-for-working-professionals/">correct your posture</a>, the iPosture will return to silently measuring you posture. If you do not correct your posture after the first alert, the iPosture will give you two vibrate in another 60 seconds.</p>
<p><strong>Easy To Use:</strong></p>
<p>All you have to do is just put this device ( iPosture) inside your shirt, or to your chest, assume the posture you want to maintain, and click the logo button on the front of the iPosture. The iPosture will vibrate once, indicating that your unique posture has been set. If you move from a standing to a sitting position, or vice versa, we suggest you reset the iPosture to each unique situation.</p>
<p><strong>Best Thing Is:</strong></p>
<p>You can reset your iPosture as often as you like during the course of a day. Reset the iPosture anytime the unit requires it, such as when moving from a standing to a seating position.</p>
