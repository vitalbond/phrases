<div><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;"><a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-vPp4m-zBFO0/U25WRRsqBHI/AAAAAAAAJ9I/kKuHKiCHG1g/s1600/DSC00454-1.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-vPp4m-zBFO0/U25WRRsqBHI/AAAAAAAAJ9I/kKuHKiCHG1g/s1600/DSC00454-1.jpg" height="400" width="300"></a></div><br>So this tutorial is only going to be of use for a small minority of sewers. However, like <a href="http://sozowhatdoyouknow.blogspot.co.uk/2012/07/maternity-tops.html" target="_blank">my maternity top pattern tutorial</a>, you may wish to bookmark it for the future! ;-) Over the last seven months I've found that attempting to dress in a stylish and comfortable way for breastfeeding/nursing can be a total pain in the arse/impossible. This is especially true if, like me, you prefer to make your own clothing rather than buy mass manufactured garments. Here's a way to adapt your favourite jersey top pattern, for example the <a href="http://shop.tillyandthebuttons.com/collections/all/products/coco" target="_blank">Tilly and the Button's Coco</a> which I've used here, for breast feeding functionality.<br><br><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;"><a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-Th3uB6ShGz4/U25WnAoTmYI/AAAAAAAAJ9Q/sQhplOaRwAI/s1600/DSC00417-1.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-Th3uB6ShGz4/U25WnAoTmYI/AAAAAAAAJ9Q/sQhplOaRwAI/s1600/DSC00417-1.jpg" height="400" width="301"></a></div><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;"><br></div><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">The basic concept of this pattern hack is to create an elasticated overlap of fabric at the boob area. In this top (or dress if you're making that version) you can discreetly 'gain access' without needing to lift up your whole garment, yank your neckline down or fiddle with any buttons or zips. You remain well covered which is a major bonus if, like me, you feel the cold or you don't enjoy exposing lots of flesh. Plus you also don't need to wear a vest layer underneath either, which I've been concerned about now we are approaching warm weather. This is an awesome answer to those two climate conundrums!&nbsp;</div><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;"><br></div><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;"><a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-e3QXFAH4rXc/U25YddSkLiI/AAAAAAAAJ9c/NGm9ChRImkU/s1600/DSC00453-1.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-e3QXFAH4rXc/U25YddSkLiI/AAAAAAAAJ9c/NGm9ChRImkU/s1600/DSC00453-1.jpg" height="400" width="298"></a></div><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;"><br></div><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">My modesty prevents me from modelling it's full function so here's my dress form instead to give you a proper idea!:</div><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;"><br></div><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;"><a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-_lfS5EeZ1-E/U25Z-00eIUI/AAAAAAAAJ9k/6ZNiRAHnILI/s1600/DSC00446-1.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-_lfS5EeZ1-E/U25Z-00eIUI/AAAAAAAAJ9k/6ZNiRAHnILI/s1600/DSC00446-1.jpg" height="400" width="300"></a></div><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;"><br></div><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;"><br></div><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;"><b>Pattern prep:</b></div><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;"><br></div><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;"><b>A note about sizing:</b>&nbsp;This pattern hack works on close fitting garments, so you may want to consider sewing a size smaller than you otherwise would, or take in the side seams a bit half way through your make. If you are in between sizes on the Coco pattern, definitely go for the smaller one. &nbsp;</div><br><b>Step 1:</b><br><br>Trace the front pattern piece for whichever Coco view you are planning to make, transferring the notches and any useful markings from the original pattern.<br><br><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;"><a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-dS_u7cOaOfE/U25eUJWPJ2I/AAAAAAAAJ94/5jz5sbHmraY/s1600/DSC00419-1.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-dS_u7cOaOfE/U25eUJWPJ2I/AAAAAAAAJ94/5jz5sbHmraY/s1600/DSC00419-1.jpg" height="300" width="400"></a></div><br><br><b>Step 2:</b><br><br>Cut the front pattern piece out and&nbsp;hold it up to your body in front of a mirror (sorry you'll have to imagine this bit in your head as doing it plus taking a photo at the same time proved impossible!). You will need to make two small marks on your pattern in pencil:<br>1) the top of your breast, roughly where the top edge of your bra cup sits (don't worry, this doesn't have to be super accurate, this line will be hidden).<br>2) your 'empire line', about 3/4 down your breast (more flattering than a line that hits right underneath your breast).<br><br><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;"><a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-tMWzlVAA1Uo/U34MLxWvWZI/AAAAAAAAKBA/cdkBq4FU7lA/s1600/DSC00420-1.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-tMWzlVAA1Uo/U34MLxWvWZI/AAAAAAAAKBA/cdkBq4FU7lA/s1600/DSC00420-1.jpg" height="298" width="400"></a></div><br><br><b>Step 3:</b><br><br>Draw lines through your marks that extend across the front pattern piece. The lines need to be at right angles to the centre front.<br><br><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;"><a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-hGFF2fGpf2o/U34NQoEdYeI/AAAAAAAAKBI/s3dmcdyNyxk/s1600/DSC00421-1.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-hGFF2fGpf2o/U34NQoEdYeI/AAAAAAAAKBI/s3dmcdyNyxk/s1600/DSC00421-1.jpg" height="300" width="400"></a></div><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;"><br></div><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;"><br></div><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;"><b>Step 4:</b></div><br>Make two new pattern pieces by tracing round your front pattern piece:<br><br>1) For the first, trace round the top part of the front pattern piece to the lower of the two lines you just drew. Add 1cm (3/8") seam allowance below the lower line. Cut the piece out. This is the new pattern piece is the upper front pattern piece and is pictured on the left below.<br><br>2) For the second, trace round the bottom part of the front pattern piece to the upper of the two lines you just drew. Add 1cm (3/8") seam allowance above the upper line. Cut the piece out. This is the new pattern piece is the lower front pattern piece and is pictured on the right below.<br><br><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;"><a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-U6h8Ktda_8w/U34NaaqX3vI/AAAAAAAAKBQ/IEjPqzbiGhQ/s1600/DSC00422-1.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-U6h8Ktda_8w/U34NaaqX3vI/AAAAAAAAKBQ/IEjPqzbiGhQ/s1600/DSC00422-1.jpg" height="300" width="400"></a></div><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;"><br></div><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;"><br></div><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;"><b>Step 5:</b></div><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;"><br></div><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">Add a notch on each of your new pattern pieces at the side seams where the other piece finishes (minus the seam allowance you added). These notches will help with the construction of the garment. You should be just be able to see them in the picture below to see what I mean:</div><br><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;"><a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-cSbWQn2lQg4/U34Nh-ujOuI/AAAAAAAAKBY/L8mUXmcX4Vc/s1600/DSC00423-1.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-cSbWQn2lQg4/U34Nh-ujOuI/AAAAAAAAKBY/L8mUXmcX4Vc/s1600/DSC00423-1.jpg" height="300" width="400"></a></div><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;"><br></div><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">Now cut out your pattern pieces in fabric as you usually would, replacing the original front pattern piece with your new upper and lower front pattern pieces.</div><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;"><br></div><b><br></b><b>Applying the elastic to the fabric pieces:</b><br><b><br></b>This stage should be completed after cutting the fabric pattern pieces out but before garment construction ensues.<br><br><b>Step 6:</b><br><br>Lay the fabric upper front piece wrong side up on a table. Leaving extra clear elastic over the edge of the side seam, pin one end of elastic to the bottom edge of the pattern piece (pictured below). Being careful not to stretch the elastic NOR allow it to be too loose, lay the elastic along the bottom edge and pin in place at the other end, once again leaving extra clear elastic over the edge of the side seam.<br><br><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;"><a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-F7cqo5NETEU/U34UZwhzlmI/AAAAAAAAKBk/eWF53AEN3CY/s1600/DSC00429-1.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-F7cqo5NETEU/U34UZwhzlmI/AAAAAAAAKBk/eWF53AEN3CY/s1600/DSC00429-1.jpg" height="298" width="400"></a></div><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;"><br></div><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;"><br></div><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;"><b>Step 7:</b></div><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;"><br></div><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">Marking with a pin, pen or whatever method you prefer, make the elastic 1cm (3/8") shorter and re-pin to the lower edge. As you can see in the picture below, the elastic will now not quite lie flat against the fabric.</div><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;"><br></div><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;"><a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-US_D-DC4FPM/U34WPF2Wg5I/AAAAAAAAKBs/QvW9XcBm0tM/s1600/DSC00428-1.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-US_D-DC4FPM/U34WPF2Wg5I/AAAAAAAAKBs/QvW9XcBm0tM/s1600/DSC00428-1.jpg" height="298" width="400"></a></div><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;"><br></div><br><b>Step 8:</b><br><br>Slightly pulling the elastic as you go so no pleats or tucks appear, baste the elastic along the bottom edge of the upper front piece by stitching down the centre of the clear elastic with a straight stitch. Then fold the 1cm (3/8") seam allowance up, encasing the elastic, and stitch using your preferred stretch stitch (I've used a three-step zigzag here).<br><br><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;"><a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-UpR6h4NkCUw/U34XLKZ32aI/AAAAAAAAKB0/qb4iv0S04ow/s1600/DSC00431-1.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-UpR6h4NkCUw/U34XLKZ32aI/AAAAAAAAKB0/qb4iv0S04ow/s1600/DSC00431-1.jpg" height="300" width="400"></a></div><br><br><b>Step 9:</b><br><br>Repeat Steps 6, 7 and 8 to apply elastic to the top edge of the lower front piece, <b>HOWEVER</b>&nbsp;make the elastic 2cms shorter in length than the top edge (unlike the 1cm you shortened the elastic on the upper front piece). The elastic applied to these edges prevents them from gapping as long as the overall top is a snug fit. The elastic on the upper front edge isn't quite as tight as the elastic on the lower front piece because a gathered effect on the upper front piece is less desirable as it would be more visible.<br><br><br><b>Garment Construction:</b><br><br>Now you can continue the construction of your garment as per the pattern instructions using the upper front piece instead of the old front piece to complete the shoulder seams, neckline/collar finishing and sleeve head insertion.<br><br><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;"><a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-uvrQVjHiQps/U34aNUO1EPI/AAAAAAAAKB8/oNt4fDOUalQ/s1600/DSC00432-1.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-uvrQVjHiQps/U34aNUO1EPI/AAAAAAAAKB8/oNt4fDOUalQ/s1600/DSC00432-1.jpg" height="298" width="400"></a></div><br><br><b>Step 10:</b><br><br>When you are ready to stitch your sleeve seams and side seams (usually done in the same process), you need to bring your lower front piece to the party. Lay the lower front piece face down on top of the upper front piece so they create an overlap. This is where those little notches to made in the side seams really help you to align the pieces correctly.<br><br><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;"><a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-Nqp_nS9yUrs/U34cd49vX6I/AAAAAAAAKCI/3mjU02PdMA4/s1600/DSC00433-1.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-Nqp_nS9yUrs/U34cd49vX6I/AAAAAAAAKCI/3mjU02PdMA4/s1600/DSC00433-1.jpg" height="298" width="400"></a></div><br><br>With the sleeve and side seams stitched, now would be a really good time to pop the garment on and see how it's fitting. As I've said before, the overlap will only function well and look nice and gape-free if the fit of the garment is quite snug. If it looks like it might be gaping, pinch in the side seams to see how much extra needs to be removed from the side seams. Withe the fit perfected, you can finish the construction of your garment.<br><br>Now you're ready for a hungry baby!<br><br><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;"><a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-je8Ennmk6ug/U25bFp_xiEI/AAAAAAAAJ9s/IxWmv-jg8rY/s1600/DSC00460-1.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-je8Ennmk6ug/U25bFp_xiEI/AAAAAAAAJ9s/IxWmv-jg8rY/s1600/DSC00460-1.jpg" height="400" width="300"></a></div><br></div>
