                    <p>The International Center for Research on Women (ICRW) is a private, nonprofit organization founded in 1976, and headquartered in Washington, DC with a mission to empower women, advance gender equality and fight poverty in the developing world.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>
<p>ICRW has an immediate need for a professional with strong qualitative and quantitative research skills and experience in adolescent sexual and reproductive health, specifically in South Asia, for the position of Research Coordinator on a study of the key barriers to adolescent sexual and reproductive health (ASRH) in urban Dhaka.&nbsp; This research project will fill critical knowledge gaps and improve the understanding of the key barriers and gendered normative influences that impact ASRH in urban Dhaka, focused mainly on the urban poor residing in low-income, informal residence communities. &nbsp;&nbsp;</p>
<p>The position will be based in Washington, D.C., with one or two trips to Dhaka, Bangladesh.&nbsp; It reports to the Senior Director of Gender, Population and Development, who also serves as the Project Director. &nbsp;</p>
<p>This is a part-time, temporary position (25-35% level of effort). Depending on need and availability of funding, there may be opportunities for additional research projects (potentially covering different thematic areas and/or regions) to increase the total LOE over time.</p>
<p><strong><em>Due to the urgency, please submit applications as soon as possible, and no later than August 28, 2013.&nbsp;</em></strong></p>        </div>

<div class="field field-type-text field-field-duties">
      <div class="field-label">Primary Duties:&nbsp;</div>
    <div class="field-items">
            <div class="field-item odd">
                    <ul><li>Assist Project Director in completing a desk review on adolescent sexual and reproductive health (ASRH) in urban Dhaka, including a summary of policies, recent research, a mapping of donor engagement and service provision, and an identification of gaps.&nbsp;</li>
<li>Work with local data collection firm and ICRW researchers to develop quantitative and qualitative research design, approach, tools, methods and study instruments.</li>
<li>Lead a research team in the design of data collection approach and oversee the administration of a household-based survey of approximately 300 married girls &nbsp;&lt;19 years of age to provide robust evidence on barriers to sexual and reproductive health access to services in two urban slum communities in Dhaka.</li>
<li>Lead a research team in conducting qualitative research (community mapping; focus group discussions; and in-depth interviews) in an urban slum community to assess the: a) healthcare service options and barriers for adolescent girls; and b) emerging ASRH challenges for both married and unmarried adolescent girls ages 15-19.</li>
<li>Lead a research team in the analysis of both quantitative and qualitative research findings.</li>
<li>Contribute significantly to the writing of the final report&nbsp;</li></ul></div>
