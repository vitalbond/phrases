<div><div class="field field-type-text field-field-subtitle">
    <div class="field-items">
            <div class="field-item odd">
                    Findings and Lessons from Seven Years of CARE International Balkans’ Young Men Initiative        </div>
        </div>
</div>
<div class="field field-type-text field-field-body">
    <div class="field-items">
            <div class="field-item odd">
                    <div>What does it mean to “be a man” to adolescent <span style="line-height: 1.5;">boys in the Balkans region? Can it mean keeping </span><span style="line-height: 1.5;">the peace instead of perpetrating violence? Having </span><span style="line-height: 1.5;">fun without drugs and alcohol? Practicing safe sex?</span></div><div> </div><div>For the past seven years, a coalition of local, regional,<span style="line-height: 1.5;">and international organizations has been </span><span style="line-height: 1.5;">promoting these positive masculine identities under </span><span style="line-height: 1.5;">the banner of the “Young Men Initiative” (YMI). </span><span style="line-height: 1.5;">Coordinated by CARE International Balkans, implemented </span><span style="line-height: 1.5;">by collaborating institutions in four countries, </span><span style="line-height: 1.5;">and evaluated by the International Center for </span><span style="line-height: 1.5;">Research on Women (ICRW), YMI seeks to promote </span><span style="line-height: 1.5;">a lifestyle that prioritizes good health, nonviolence, </span><span style="line-height: 1.5;">and gender equality through a combination of educational </span><span style="line-height: 1.5;">workshops and community campaigns. </span><span style="line-height: 1.5;">This synthesis report provides an overview of the </span><span style="line-height: 1.5;">program’s rationale, design, and evaluation results </span><span style="line-height: 1.5;">from YMI’s implementation in vocational high</span></div><div>schools.</div>        </div>
        </div>
</div>
