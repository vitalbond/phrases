            <div class="field-item odd">
                    A Comprehsensive Evaluation of the Gap Inc. P.A.C.E. Program        </div>
        </div>
</div>
<div class="field field-type-text field-field-body">
    <div class="field-items">
            <div class="field-item odd">
                    <p>Globally the garment industry is one of the biggest employers of low‚Äêskilled women workers.&nbsp; Despite their large numbers in the workforce, relatively few female garment workers advance to higher-level positions as they have limited opportunities to acquire the skills that would enable their professional and personal growth.&nbsp; In response to this need, Gap Inc. initiated the P.A.C.E. (Personal Advancement &amp; Career Enhancement) workplace education program to teach women the managerial, interpersonal, organizational and other practical skills needed to move forward in work and in life.&nbsp; &nbsp;</p><p>This report summarizes findings from program evaluations conducted by ICRW from 2009 - 2013 at six factory sites where P.A.C.E. is implemented - two in India and one each in Cambodia, Vietnam, Bangladesh and China.</p><p>Research findings from these robust, multi-country evaluations demonstrate that P.A.C.E. is an effective, sustainable and scalable model that yields high returns for women, their families and the businesses where they work.&nbsp;</p>        </div>
