<div><p>Hi Friends!</p>
<p>Let&rsquo;s talk cookies today, shall we? Sometimes you just need a good cookie&hellip;am I right?! I&rsquo;m all for indulging in more &ldquo;traditional&rdquo; cookies every once and while. But I also like playing around with recipes and trying to make them healthier.</p>
<p>With the big surgery tomorrow, a batch of cookies seemed pretty much necessary to me. Yes- I am a stress-baker.</p>
<p></p><center><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-12612" src="http://www.theleangreenbean.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/healthy-peanut-butter-oatmeal-cookies.jpg.jpg" alt="healthy peanut butter oatmeal cookies.jpg Healthy Peanut Butter Oatmeal Cookies" width="428" height="640" title="Healthy Peanut Butter Oatmeal Cookies"></center>A few weeks ago I was in the mood for an oatmeal cookie. I love adding peanut butter to cookies and since I&rsquo;m a huge fan of mix-ins&hellip;.I had some fun with those as well. I&rsquo;ve made several batches of these and finally got what I was looking for. They&rsquo;re&nbsp;not super sweet&hellip;but they&rsquo;re packed with healthy ingredients and still feel like a treat.
<p><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-12613" src="http://www.theleangreenbean.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/1.jpg" alt="1 Healthy Peanut Butter Oatmeal Cookies" width="550" height="368" title="Healthy Peanut Butter Oatmeal Cookies"></p>
<p>I&rsquo;ve also made these with Greek yogurt instead of butter. It&rsquo;s pretty amazing how differently they turn out- the yogurt batch is much more moist and cake-like. I stored my yogurt batch in a tupperware with the lid not sealed- otherwise they&rsquo;re too moist and they&rsquo;ll get moldy. You could also store them in the fridge. With the butter version you don&rsquo;t have to worry about it.</p>
<p>Here&rsquo;s the recipe:</p>

<div class="ERSIngredients">
<div class="ERSIngredientsHeader ERSHeading">Ingredients</div>
<ul><li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">4 Tablespoons unsalted butter *</li>
<li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">&frac12; cup peanut butter</li>
<li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">&frac14; cup brown sugar, loosely packed</li>
<li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">1 teaspoon vanilla extract</li>
<li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">2 large eggs</li>
<li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">&frac34; cup white whole wheat flour</li>
<li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">1&frac12; cups rolled oats</li>
<li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">&frac12; teaspoon baking soda</li>
<li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">1 teaspoon cinnamon</li>
<li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">2 Tablespoons chia seeds</li>
<li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">⅓ cup pumpkin seeds</li>
<li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">⅓ cup dark chocolate chips</li>
</ul><div class="ERSClear"></div>
</div>
<div class="ERSInstructions">
<div class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">Instructions</div>
<ol><li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">Preheat oven to 375 degrees.</li>
<li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">In a large bowl, melt the butter and peanut butter. Stir well to combine.</li>
<li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">Add the sugar, vanilla and eggs and mix well.</li>
<li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">Add remaining ingredients and stir until just mixed.</li>
<li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">Drop onto greased cookie sheet and flatten with fork (if desired).</li>
<li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">Bake for 9-10 minutes.</li>
</ol><div class="ERSClear"></div>
</div>
<div class="ERSNotesDiv">
<div class="ERSNotesHeader">Notes</div>
<div class="ERSNotes">* You can substitute &frac12; cup Greek yogurt for the butter if you&rsquo;d like a more moist, cake-like cookie.<br>This recipe makes 2 dozen cookies. If you make them mini-sized you&rsquo;ll get about 3 dozen.<br>These are not meant to be overly sweet. If they&rsquo;re not sweet enough for you, add another &frac14; cup sugar or some dried fruit.</div>

<p>Like I mentioned in the notes, this recipe makes about 2 dozen cookies. If you make them mini, you&rsquo;ll get about 3 dozen. That&rsquo;s what I did here.</p>
<p><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-12619" src="http://www.theleangreenbean.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/peanut-butter-oatmeal-cookies.jpg" alt="peanut butter oatmeal cookies Healthy Peanut Butter Oatmeal Cookies" width="550" height="550" title="Healthy Peanut Butter Oatmeal Cookies"></p>
<p>The batch pictured above used Greek yogurt. Can you see the texture difference?! This is also the batch I made mini&hellip;because FACT- if you make cookies mini, you can eat more of them.</p>
<p>If you don&rsquo;t smash them with a fork before baking, they&rsquo;ll just be very tall and rounded. I typically prefer them flatter so I like to smash them. Plus it&rsquo;s fun!</p>
<p></p><center><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-12614" src="http://www.theleangreenbean.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/healthy-peanut-butter-oatmeal-cookies-2.jpg.jpg" alt="healthy peanut butter oatmeal cookies 2.jpg Healthy Peanut Butter Oatmeal Cookies" width="428" height="640" title="Healthy Peanut Butter Oatmeal Cookies"></center>If you don&rsquo;t have pumpkin seeds, you could try adding some chopped nuts! If they&rsquo;re not sweet enough for you, add another 1/4 cup sugar or some dried fruit!
<p><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-12615" src="http://www.theleangreenbean.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/healthy-peanut-butter-oatmeal-cookies-3.jpg.jpg" alt="healthy peanut butter oatmeal cookies 3.jpg Healthy Peanut Butter Oatmeal Cookies" width="550" height="375" title="Healthy Peanut Butter Oatmeal Cookies"></p>
