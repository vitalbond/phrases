<p>It&rsquo;s time for another <a href="http://thereciperedux.com/" target="_blank">Recipe Redux</a> post. Look at me go, I&rsquo;m posting on time, on the right day and everything! Anyways, here&rsquo;s the prompt for this month:</p>
<p style="color: #6e5300;"><span style="color: #ff6600;"><strong>Cooking with Tea</strong></span></p>
<p style="color: #6e5300;"><span style="color: #ff6600;">Tea cups around the world are bubbling up with bold new flavors: From cardamom chai and sencha green to bubble teas and veggie teas. We wish we could attend the World Tea Expo the end of this month &ndash; but in lieu of a plane ticket, we&rsquo;ll be cooking and baking and stirring up tea-inspired healthy dishes.</span></p>
<p style="color: #6e5300;"><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-12373" src="http://www.theleangreenbean.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/green-tea-rice-5.jpg.jpg" alt="green tea rice 5.jpg {Recipe Redux} Green Tea Rice" width="550" height="406" title="{Recipe Redux} Green Tea Rice"></p>
<p style="color: #6e5300;">I&rsquo;m not a very adventurous tea drinker. I basically drink peppermint tea when I&rsquo;m sick and then several years ago I got on a green tea kick. Before I even started trying to get pregnant, I switched from the small amount of coffee I was drinking, to decaf green tea. That was my go-to for a while&hellip;and then I switched to just drinking hot water with grated ginger and a slice of lemon if I wanted something hot to drink.</p>
<p style="color: #6e5300;">Now I mostly just drink water, but I still had a stash of green tea in the cupboard so I decided to use that for my recipe. I went the simple route this month. This recipe is nothing fancy. It popped in my head while I was cooking some brown rice for food prep a few weeks ago. Typically I just cook my grains in water, but I have occasionally cooked them in chicken or veggie broth for some extra flavor. I decided to try cooking my next batch of rice in some green tea to impart a subtle flavor and it worked perfectly!</p>
