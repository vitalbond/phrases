<div><p>Hi Friends!</p>
<p>Just popping in with a quick new recipe to share today! As you probably know, I eat oatmeal pretty much every morning for breakfast. Usually I just make it in the microwave, but yesterday I thought it might be fun to change things up and make a baked version.</p>
<p><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-12033" alt="2 Baked Oatmeal" src="http://www.theleangreenbean.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/2.jpg" width="550" height="380" title="Baked Oatmeal"></p>
<p>This is a twist on my favorite morning oatmeal combo, with a few adjustments to make it more suitable for baking! Keep in mind that this is not very sweet. It works for me with just the sweetness from the banana and milk and then the fruit and pb drizzle on top. If you like things sweeter, you may want to add a little honey or sugar to yours.</p>
<p>Here&rsquo;s the recipe:</p>

<div class="ERSIngredients">
<div class="ERSIngredientsHeader ERSHeading">Ingredients</div>
<ul><li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">1 cup rolled oats</li>
<li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">&frac34; cup milk</li>
<li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">1 egg</li>
<li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">&frac14; cup Dark Chocolate Nuttzo (or your favorite nut butter)</li>
<li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">&frac14; cup mashed banana</li>
<li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">&frac14; cup raw pumpkin seeds</li>
<li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">&frac12; teaspoon baking soda</li>
<li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">1 teaspoon cinnamon</li>
<li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">Toppings: additional nut butter &amp; fruit</li>
</ul><div class="ERSClear"></div></div>
<div class="ERSInstructions">
<div class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">Instructions</div>
<ol><li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">Mash the banana and Nuttzo (or nut butter) together in a small bowl.</li>
<li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">Add the oats, milk and egg and mix well.</li>
<li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">Stir in the baking soda, cinnamon and pumpkin seeds.</li>
<li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">Pour into large, greased ramekins (or whatever pan you want to use) and bake at 400 degrees for 25 minutes.</li>
<li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">Top with a drizzle of nut butter and some fresh fruit if desired.</li>
</ol><div class="ERSClear"></div></div>
<div class="ERSNotesDiv">
<div class="ERSNotesHeader">Notes</div>
<div class="ERSNotes">You could double this recipe and bake in a bigger pan.<br>Lining the bottom of the ramekins or pan with parchment paper makes it easy to remove.</div>
