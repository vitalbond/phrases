<p>But first, let’s recap why almonds are so awesome! In <span style="text-decoration: underline;"><strong>one serving of almonds (1 oz), which amounts to 23 whole almonds</strong></span>, there is:</p>
<ul><li>6g protein</li>
<li>4g fiber</li>
<li>9g monounsaturated fat</li>
<li>Vitamin E (35% DV) – important antioxidant that helps protect cells from damage &#38; promote healthy skin and hair</li>
<li>Riboflavin (20% DV) – important for red blood cell production</li>
<li>Magnesium (20% DV) – helps regulate muscle &#38; nerve function, blood sugar levels and blood pressure</li>
<li>Phosphorus (15% DV) – helps build strong bones &#38; teeth</li>
<li>Plus calcium, potassium, niacin and iron</li>
</ul><p>That’s a whole lot of goodness packed into these little nuts!</p>

<h3 style="text-align: center;"><span style="text-decoration: underline;"><strong>Almond Butter</strong></span></h3>
<p style="text-align: center;"><img class="aligncenter wp-image-12747 size-full" src="http://www.theleangreenbean.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/almond-butter.jpg.jpg" alt="almond butter.jpg My Favorite Ways To Eat Almonds" width="550" height="368" title="My Favorite Ways To Eat Almonds"></p>
<p style="text-align: center;">Current fav flavor combo:<br><span style="text-decoration: underline;"><strong>Homemade Honey Vanilla Cinnamon</strong></span></p>
<p style="text-align: center;">2 cups raw almonds +1 tsp honey + 1/2 tsp cinnamon + 1/2 tsp vanilla<br>
Place in high powered blender or food processor.<br>
Process until smooth.<br>
Taste and add more honey, cinnamon or vanilla if desired.</p>
<p> </p>
<p><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-12761" src="http://www.theleangreenbean.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/granola-bar.jpg.jpg" alt="granola bar.jpg My Favorite Ways To Eat Almonds" width="513" height="368" title="My Favorite Ways To Eat Almonds"></p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><span style="text-decoration: underline;"><strong>No-Bake Granola Bars</strong></span></p>
<p style="text-align: center;">Combine 1 cups rolled oats + 1 cup cereal + 1/4 cup pumpkin seeds + 1/4 cup chopped almonds + 1/4 cup chocolate chips + 2 Tbsp chia seeds in a large bowl.<br>
Melt 1/2 cup almond butter + 1/3 cup honey and mix well.<br>
Add wet to dry, mix until well coated (use your hands if you have to).<br>
Dump into a 9×9 inch pan, press to flatten and cover bottom.<br>
Freeze for 30 minutes, cut into bars and enjoy.<br>
Store in the fridge.</p>

<p style="text-align: center;"><span style="text-decoration: underline;"><strong>Almond-Crusted Chicken</strong> <strong>Tenders</strong></span></p>
<p style="text-align: center;">Mix 1 cup almond meal with your favorite spices.<br>
I used paprika, garlic powder, black pepper &#38; cayenne.<br>
*Tip- make your own by simply pulsing<br>
whole almonds in the blender or food processor*<br>
Cut 1 pound of chicken breasts into tenders (or use store bought).<br>
Dip tenders in egg wash, then press into almond meal mixture.<br>
Set a wire cooling rack on a baking sheet.<br>
Place the chicken tenders on the rack.<br>
Bake at 400 degrees F for 20 minutes.</p>
