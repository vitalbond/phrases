<div><p>Hi Friends!</p>
<p>This isn&rsquo;t the post I was planning to share today&hellip;but the other night I got this craving for a dutch baby and I couldn&rsquo;t get it out of my head&hellip;so I made one for lunch yesterday, snapped a few pics and thought I&rsquo;d share it with you today. If you&rsquo;ve never heard of a dutch baby&hellip;it&rsquo;s also known as a German pancake. At a basic level, there&rsquo;s really not much too it&hellip;it&rsquo;s just some eggs, milk, a little flour and a little sugar. I remember eating them as a little kid but for some reason I rarely make them now.</p>
<p><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-11967" alt="dutch baby 1 Apple Cinnamon Dutch Baby" src="http://www.theleangreenbean.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/dutch-baby-1.jpg" width="550" height="368" title="Apple Cinnamon Dutch Baby"></p>
<p>The thing that really makes dutch babies so awesome is the toppings and mix-ins you choose. There&rsquo;s not a ton of flavor in the &ldquo;pancake&rdquo; part&hellip;but I view it mainly as a vehicle for the toppings. They&rsquo;re great with fresh fruit and yogurt in the summertime (or anytime, really&hellip;) &ndash; I especially love them with peaches! This time, I decided to go the apple cinnamon route.</p>
<p>Here&rsquo;s what you need:</p>

<div class="ERSServes">Serves: <span itemprop="recipeYield">2-3</span></div></div>
<div class="ERSIngredients">
<div class="ERSIngredientsHeader ERSHeading">Ingredients</div>
<ul><li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">3 eggs</li>
<li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">&frac12; cup milk</li>
<li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">&frac12; cup flour (I use bread flour)</li>
<li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">&frac12; teaspoon vanilla</li>
<li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">&frac12; teaspoon cinnamon</li>
<li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">1 Tablespoon sugar</li>
<li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">2 Tablespoons butter</li>
<li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">1 small apple, diced</li>
<li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">2 Tablespoons peanut butter</li>
<li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">handful of chocolate chips</li>
</ul><div class="ERSClear"></div></div>
<div class="ERSInstructions">
<div class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">Instructions</div>
<ol><li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">Place a cast iron skillet or other oven-safe pan in the oven and preheat to 425 degrees F.</li>
<li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">In a small bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk and vanilla until frothy.</li>
<li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">Add the flour and cinnamon and whisk for 2-3 minutes until well mixed.</li>
<li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">Dice apple and set aside.</li>
<li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">Once oven is preheated, carefully remove the hot pan, add butter and swirl around pan until bottom and sides are coated.</li>
<li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">Add apples to the bottom of the pan.</li>
<li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">Pour batter into pan, tilt until whole pan is covered and return to oven.</li>
<li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">Bake for 18-20 minutes, watching carefully at the end so the edges don&rsquo;t burn.</li>
<li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">Remove from oven, top with peanut butter and chocolate chips and serve warm.</li>
</ol><div class="ERSClear"></div></div>
<div class="endeasyrecipe" title="style002" style="display: none">3.2.2310</div></div>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Here&rsquo;s a look at it just out of the oven. It will be puffy when it comes out and then deflate slightly. I used bread flour because I read somewhere that it makes them the puffiest, but you could also try with AP flour&hellip;or even whole wheat flour but it might not puff as much.</p>
<p><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-11968" alt="dutch baby 2 Apple Cinnamon Dutch Baby" src="http://www.theleangreenbean.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/dutch-baby-2.jpg" width="550" height="364" title="Apple Cinnamon Dutch Baby"></p>
<p>Like I said, it&rsquo;s the toppings that really make this&hellip;so get creative! Pretty much any fruit is delicious, add more cinnamon or vanilla if you like it, drizzle with syrup, fruit sauce, etc.</p>
<p><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-11969" alt="dutch baby 3 Apple Cinnamon Dutch Baby" src="http://www.theleangreenbean.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/dutch-baby-3.jpg" width="550" height="368" title="Apple Cinnamon Dutch Baby"></p>
<p>These make the perfect treat for a weekend breakfast. If you want to serve more people, you could try doubling the recipe so it&rsquo;s a bit thicker. (I haven&rsquo;t tried that personally though, so no promises!)</p>
