<p>Time for another recipe refresher! I first shared these <a href="http://www.theleangreenbean.com/thai-peanut-salmon-burgers/" target="_blank">Thai Peanut Salmon Burgers</a> a couple of years ago after I made them on a whim&hellip;but then I kinda forgot about them.</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-11926" title="thai peanut salmon burgers" alt="DSC 0648 {Refreshed} Thai Peanut Salmon Burgers" src="http://www.theleangreenbean.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/DSC_0648.jpg" width="550" height="368"></p>
<p>Last month I whipped them up again and remembered why I love them. They&rsquo;re great the night you make them&hellip;but they&rsquo;re even better as leftovers. They seem to stick together better once they&rsquo;ve cooled and reheated them.</p>
<p>I make these with fresh/frozen salmon but canned would also probably work.</p>
<p>Here&rsquo;s what you need:</p>

<div class="ERSIngredients">
<div class="ERSIngredientsHeader ERSHeading">Ingredients</div>
<ul><li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">1 lb fresh salmon</li>
<li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">&frac12; c sliced green onion</li>
<li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">2 Tbsp low sodium teryaki or soy sauce</li>
<li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">2 Tbsp sweet chili sauce</li>
<li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">1 Tbsp natural peanut butter</li>
<li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">1 tsp fresh ground ginger</li>
<li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">&frac14; c plain, non-fat Greek yogurt</li>
<li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">&frac14; c lightly salted peanuts, roughly chopped</li>
<li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">&frac12; c panko breadcrumbs</li>
</ul><div class="ERSClear"></div></div>
<div class="ERSInstructions">
<div class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">Instructions</div>
<ol><li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">Cook salmon in a hot pan until cooked through. Flake with a fork and let cool slightly. (You could also used pre-cooked salmon or canned salmon)</li>
<li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">In a medium-sized bowl, combine all ingredients and stir until well-mixed.</li>
<li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">Form into patties, squeezing tightly to form (don&rsquo;t make them too big!) and place in the freezer while the oven preheats to 425 degrees F. (You could also cook these in a pan, but I would still recommend freezing them for at least 15 minutes).</li>
<li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">Place on a well-greased baking sheet (or try a silpat or parchment paper).</li>
<li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">Cook 18 minutes, flipping once.</li>
</ol><div class="ERSClear"></div></div>
