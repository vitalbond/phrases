<div><p>Hi Friends!</p>
<p>Something you may not know about me…. I spent my summers in high school and college working for a woman who owned her own landscaping company. The hours were long- we worked from 6AM to 4PM, but the pay was great &#38; I got to work with all my closest friends. My boss was, in a word, crazy. In a few other words- eccentric, unconventional and a little kooky. But she had a good heart.</p>
<p><img class="aligncenter wp-image-12697 size-full" src="http://www.theleangreenbean.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/blueberry-muffin-2.jpg.jpg" alt="blueberry muffin 2.jpg Blueberry Muffins" width="550" height="777" title="Blueberry Muffins"></p>
<p>She would leave frozen Gatorade for the trash men every week when then temperatures climbed above 100 degrees, she started everyone who worked for her at $12/hr and gave us all a $1/hr raise for every summer we came back, and if we were working on a particularly hard job, she’d give us her credit card and send us to the local grocery store to cool down and buy freshly baked muffins.</p>
<p><img class="aligncenter wp-image-12698 size-full" src="http://www.theleangreenbean.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/blueberry-muffin-4.jpg" alt="blueberry muffin 4 Blueberry Muffins" width="550" height="368" title="Blueberry Muffins"></p><p style="text-align: center;">Muffin days were the best days. And blueberry muffins were my favorite.</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-12699" src="http://www.theleangreenbean.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/blueberry-muffin-3.jpg" alt="blueberry muffin 3 Blueberry Muffins" width="550" height="822" title="Blueberry Muffins"></p>
<p>Thus, the reason for this post. I’ve made a lot of blueberry muffins in my day and these that I’m about to share with you might be the best batch yet. They’re big &#38; fluffy, packed with blueberries and not overly sweet.</p>
<p>Here’s the recipe for ya!</p>

<div class="ERSIngredients">
<div class="ERSIngredientsHeader ERSHeading">Ingredients</div>
<ul><li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">2 Tablespoons butter</li>
<li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">1 egg</li>
<li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">⅔ cup plain yogurt (whole milk or 2%)</li>
<li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">1 teaspoon vanilla extract</li>
<li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">¾ cup white whole wheat flour</li>
<li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">¾ cup rolled oats</li>
<li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">⅓ cup sugar</li>
<li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">½ teaspoon baking powder</li>
<li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">½ teaspoon baking soda</li>
<li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">1 teaspoon cinnamon</li>
<li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">⅔ cup fresh blueberries</li>
<li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">Sugar for sprinkling (optional)</li>
</ul><div class="ERSClear"></div>
</div>
<div class="ERSInstructions">
<div class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">Instructions</div>
<ol><li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">Preheat oven to 350 degrees F.</li>
<li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">Melt butter in microwave.</li>
<li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">Stir in egg, yogurt and vanilla until well combined.</li>
<li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">Add flour, oats, sugar, baking powder, baking soda and cinnamon and stir until just combined.</li>
<li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">Gently mix in berries, reserving a tablespoon or two.</li>
<li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">Scoop in greased or lined muffin tins. Fill the tins most of the way full- you should get 7-8 muffins.</li>
<li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">Press a few berries into the top of each muffin and sprinkle lightly with sugar if desired.</li>
<li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">Bake for 25 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.</li>
</ol><div class="ERSClear"></div>
</div>
<div class="ERSNotesDiv">
<div class="ERSNotesHeader">Notes</div>
<div class="ERSNotes">You can substitute frozen berries if you’d like. Just roll in a little flour and mix very gently so all your batter doesn’t turn purple!</div>

<p>Saving a few blueberries to press into the top of each one makes them look extra fancy…and sprinkling with just a pinch of sugar on top is crucial, in my opinion!</p>
<p><img class="aligncenter wp-image-12700 size-full" src="http://www.theleangreenbean.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/blueberry-muffin-1.jpg" alt="blueberry muffin 1 Blueberry Muffins" width="550" height="368" title="Blueberry Muffins"></p>
<p>This recipe makes 7-8 large muffins. They’re very moist, so I store them loosely covered instead of sealing them in an airtight container. If you aren’t going to eat them within a couple of days, I’d recommend storing them in the fridge.</p>
<p><img class="aligncenter wp-image-12701 size-full" src="http://www.theleangreenbean.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/blueberry-muffin-5.jpg" alt="blueberry muffin 5 Blueberry Muffins" width="550" height="822" title="Blueberry Muffins"></p>
<p>But let’s be honest…they’re probably not going to last that long!</p>
<p>Enjoy!<br>
