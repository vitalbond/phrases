<div><p>Hi Friends!</p>
<p>As promised, I&rsquo;m here today with a sweet treat to share. This is a family recipe that my mom used to make all the time when I was growing up. She got it from her mom. I don&rsquo;t know where it came from before that.</p>
<p><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-12268" src="http://www.theleangreenbean.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/black-bottom-cupcakes.jpg" alt="black bottom cupcakes Black Bottom Cupcakes" width="550" height="384" title="Black Bottom Cupcakes"></p>
<p>My mimi called these cupcakes Girdle Snappers. For the purpose of this post, I&rsquo;m calling them Black Bottom Cupcakes&hellip;because it does a better job explaining what they are! They&rsquo;re half cheesecake, half cupcake, all delicious.</p>
<p>They&rsquo;re not super healthy but I did make a few changes to the original recipe to cut back on the sugar a bit.</p>

The recipe said this should make 12-15 cupcakes. I made 13 before I ran out of the cheesecake mixture, so I just added some extra chocolate chips made 4 plain chocolate cupcakes with the remaining batter. I think I could have done a better job of dividing evenly.
<p><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-12269" src="http://www.theleangreenbean.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/DSC_0108.jpg" alt="DSC 0108 Black Bottom Cupcakes" width="440" height="640" title="Black Bottom Cupcakes"></p>
<p>Like I mentioned in the notes above, I used some of hubby&rsquo;s leftover morning coffee to replace some of the water in the original recipe for added flavor. If you don&rsquo;t have coffee, just leave it out and use 1 cup of water..or you could probably do half milk, half water.</p>
<p><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-12270" src="http://www.theleangreenbean.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/black-bottom-cupcakes-1.jpg.jpg" alt="black bottom cupcakes 1.jpg Black Bottom Cupcakes" width="444" height="640" title="Black Bottom Cupcakes"></p>
<p>They&rsquo;re best eaten cold, so if you can, make them a day ahead of time and store them in the fridge!</p>
<p>Enjoy!<br>
